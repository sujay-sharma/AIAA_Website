Projects

Design Build Fly


The American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics’ Design Build Fly is an annual international competition sponsored by various aerospace companies and held in Tucson, Arizona. The competition challenges students to fabricate an RC aircraft to be evaluated through various missions that are changed each year. Criteria of past missions have included carrying simulated passengers and payloads and executing difficult maneuvers in flight. Ninety-one teams from around the world competed in the 2018 competition, with the number of participants increasing every year.

Cansat


The 2018 CanSat competition objective was to design a rocket payload with an aero-braking heat shield. After being violently ejected from the rocket payload section, the probe executes the following:
• Aerodynamically orients itself
• Deploys heat shield to control descent
• Detaches heat shield as it deploys a parachute
• Lands on ground while keeping delicate payload intact
During descent, telemetry and atmospheric data is transmitted real-time to ground station to be displayed on a Heads-Up Display (HUD). The data being displayed includes: Probe Orientation (x-y-z tilt), GPS Location (Longitude + Latitude), Altitude (calculated from pressure), Pressure, Temperature, Humidity, Battery Output Voltage.

RAD


The Research Aerospace Design program, otherwise known as RAD, was started in August 2018 with the explicit goal to provide 24 selected members with valuable team experience and to introduce them to basic electronic design. RAD is a two-semester long program, with the Fall semester dedicated to weekly two-hour long workshops where members are taught about designing and implementing electronics in aircraft. The workshops themselves are split between a lecture section and a lab section where members directly apply what they learned through building small scale circuits to power LEDs to more robust designs that involve using a microcontroller and a shield for operating brushless motors.
